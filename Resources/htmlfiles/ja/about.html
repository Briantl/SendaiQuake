The 2011 Sendai earthquake and tsunami was a 9.0MW megathrust earthquake off the coast of Japan that occurred at 05:46 UTC (14:46 local time) on 11 March 2011. The epicenter was reported to be 130 kilometers (81 miles) off the east coast of the Oshika Peninsula, T≈çhoku, with the hypocenter at a depth of 24.4 kilometers (15.2 miles).

The earthquake triggered tsunami warnings and evacuations from Japan's Pacific coast and at least 20 countries, including the entire Pacific coast of North America and South America. The earthquake created tsunami waves of up to 10 meters (33 feet) that struck Japan, with smaller waves in many other countries. In Japan, the waves are reported to have travelled up to 10 kilometers (6 miles) inland.
